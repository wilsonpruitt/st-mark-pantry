What it is

The same notebook your pantry already uses, but kept by the computer.

Offline first

Runs on a Chromebook with the wifi out. Data lives on the device and syncs back to the cloud when the connection returns.

Built for shared devices

One device, many volunteers. Sign in once at setup; a simple between-shifts password locks the screen.

TEFAP-ready

Optional compliance mode adds signature capture, FPL eligibility, annual recertification, and the monthly USDA report.

Who it’s for

Small feeding ministries that want a record, not a CRM.

  • Food pantries operating one to three days a week
  • Soup kitchens and prepared-meal programs
  • Backpack programs distributing to schools
  • Mobile pantries and rural distributions
  • Operations between 50 and 5,000 households
  • Programs that share devices across volunteers
  • Ministries reporting to TEFAP or a regional food bank
  • Small standalone pantries with no compliance load

Cupboard began as the system for one pantry in Austin, Texas, kept on a single Chromebook, with no IT staff and no budget. It is a record of what a small ministry actually needs — not what enterprise software thinks it should.
