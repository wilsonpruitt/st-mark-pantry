Cupboard is software for small feeding ministries who want a record of what they do
without buying a Salesforce contract or learning a database. It works offline, syncs
when it can, handles TEFAP if you need it, and disappears when you don't.

Pantries, soup kitchens, backpack programs — kept on any laptop or tablet, optimized
for the Chromebook most pantries already own.

## What it is

**Offline first.** Runs on any laptop or tablet, even with the wifi out. Data lives on
the device and syncs back to the cloud when the connection returns.

**Built for shared devices.** One device, many volunteers. Sign in once at setup; a
simple between-shifts password locks the screen.

**TEFAP-ready.** An optional compliance mode adds signature capture, FPL eligibility,
annual recertification, and the monthly USDA report.

## Who it's for

\begin{multicols}{2}
\begin{itemize}
\item Food pantries operating one to three days a week
\item Soup kitchens and prepared-meal programs
\item Backpack programs distributing to schools
\item Mobile pantries and rural distributions
\item Operations between 50 and 5,000 households
\item Programs that share devices across volunteers
\item Ministries reporting to TEFAP or a regional food bank
\item Small standalone pantries with no compliance load
\end{itemize}
\end{multicols}

\pullquote{Cupboard began as the system for one pantry in Austin, Texas, kept on a single Chromebook, with no IT staff and no budget. It is a record of what a small ministry actually needs — not what enterprise software thinks it should.}
